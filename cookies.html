Cookies in Websites

HTTP cookies are small name‚Äìvalue pairs (a fundamental data representation in computer systems and) stored by the browser. They allow users to enable sessions, preferences, analytics, and more. The table below contrasts common categories.

Category Purpose Examples Consent? Typical Duration
Strictly Necessary Core site functionality (auth sessions, CSRF, load balancing). sessionid, csrftoken, routing/sticky cookies. Generally not consent-gated (still disclose). Session or short-term.
Performance Improves speed and reliability (CDN metrics, error tracking). RUM beacons, crash IDs. Often consent-gated depending on jurisdiction. Short to medium.
Functional Remember choices (language, theme, accessibility). theme=dark, locale, UI prefs. Usually requires consent or user action. Medium to long.
Analytics Aggregated usage measurement and engagement. GA/PLA IDs, first-party analytics IDs. Consent required in many regions (e.g., EU). Medium to long.

Summary. It is better to keep strictly necessary cookies minimal and mostly documented. Also, it is important to gate non-essential cookies (performance, functional, analytics,...) behind consent when required, offer easy revocation, and prefer first-party, short-lived identifiers with clear retention policies.
