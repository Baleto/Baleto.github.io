Search Engine Optimization (SEO) ensures that pages can be discovered, crawled, indexed, and ranked correctly ‚Äî and that their content aligns with the user‚Äôs search intent. Below is a comparison of how major search engines treat core indexing directives.

Engine Crawl Agent robots.txt Meta robots Canonical Sitemaps
Google Googlebot Respected. Disallow blocks crawling; known URLs may still be indexed without content. index/noindex, follow/nofollow, noarchive. Preferred signal for duplicate/variant consolidation. /sitemap.xml, and robots.txt discovery supported.
Bing Bingbot Respected similarly to Google. Same directives; also supports snippet-length rules like max-snippet. Supported. Supported; can also submit through Bing Webmaster Tools.
DuckDuckGo (uses Bing + partners) Follows upstream crawlers' robots rules. Inherited from upstream indexing sources. Canonical respected according to upstream signals. Same sitemap signals as upstream engines.
Applebot Applebot Respected. Supports standard indexing directives. Supported. Supported.

Summary. robots.txt governs crawling, not indexing; <meta name="robots"> governs indexing + link-following; <link rel="canonical"> consolidates duplicates and ranking signals; XML sitemaps increase discoverability. Clean information architecture and strong content remain foundational.
